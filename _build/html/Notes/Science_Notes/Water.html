Molecular interaction
Geometry

Best way to study intermolecular interaction is in the solid phase, at low temerature (reduce molecular movement ‚Ä¶.)

The basic principles that govern the arrangement of atoms in water ice are called the ice rules (or Bernal‚ÄìFowler rules) and they state that each oxygen is covalently bonded to two hydrogen atoms and that the oxygen atom in each water molecule forms two hydrogen bonds with other water molecules. As a result, each water molecule is embeded in a teraedron formed by four neighbouring molecules, called a Walrafen pentamer

Fig. 4 Explain: source MC¬∂

This tetrahedral geometry is caused by the ability of water to form 4 hydrogen bonds with other water molecules:

  • 2 donors (D) from the 2 hydrogens

  • 2 acceptors (A) from the Oxygen lone pairs of electrons
